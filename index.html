Assessment Checklist

Toilet Needs & Urgency

Key Points to Mention

  • Extreme urgency - need toilet within 30 seconds when urgency hits
  • Multiple embarrassing accidents in public when unable to find facilities in time
  • Internal bleeding during flares
  • Frequency - need to use toilet 10+ times daily during flares
  • Unpredictable - cannot predict when urgency will occur
  • May need to interrupt activities (eating, bathing, dressing) for urgent toilet needs

Specific Examples

Public Accident Example

Describe a specific instance when you had an accident in public due to not finding facilities in time. Include details about the location, the distress it caused, and the impact it had on your confidence afterwards.

Work Example

Explain how toilet urgency affects your work day - having to suddenly leave meetings or tasks, concerns about explaining frequent toilet visits to colleagues, and the stress this causes.

Home Example

Describe how toilet urgency disrupts home activities - for example, having to rush from the shower, interrupting meal preparation, or waking multiple times at night.

PIP Assessment Relevance

This directly relates to the "Using the toilet and managing incontinence" activity in the PIP assessment. Your 30-second urgency window and accidents are crucial evidence for limitations in this area.

Communication Tip

Be direct and specific about toilet accidents - while this may feel embarrassing, these details are essential for conveying the severity of your condition. Use precise timeframes (e.g., "within 30 seconds") and frequencies.
